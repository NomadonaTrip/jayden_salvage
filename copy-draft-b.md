## Annotations: Key Decisions

1. **Shifted from "we do" to "you get" language throughout.** The current copy describes what the business does. The new copy describes what the customer walks away with.

2. **Added specificity wherever possible.** "50–70% below dealer retail" is more convincing than "a fraction of dealer cost." A registration number is more convincing than "registered."

3. **Maintained the industrial, no-nonsense tone.** This isn't a tech startup. The copy should sound like a handshake, not a pitch deck.

4. **Addressed objections directly.** "Running or not." "No cheque, no wait." "No phone trees." Every line pre-empts a reason not to call.

5. **Created urgency through truth, not pressure.** "That car isn't getting more valuable" is honest — vehicles depreciate and rust. No fake countdown timers or "limited time" nonsense.
